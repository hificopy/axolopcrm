# Weekly Feature Rollout Marketing Guide

## Overview

Axolop CRM ships **one new feature every Monday** at 9:00 AM EST. Each feature gets a full marketing push to drive adoption and showcase our rapid innovation.

## Weekly Marketing Timeline

### Monday (Launch Day)

- **9:00 AM EST** - Feature goes live
- **9:15 AM EST** - Email blast to all users
- **10:00 AM EST** - Social media posts across all platforms
- **11:00 AM EST** - Blog post published
- **2:00 PM EST** - Video demo released on YouTube

### Tuesday-Friday (Follow-up)

- Daily social media posts with use cases
- User testimonials and screenshots
- Tips and tricks content
- Community engagement and Q&A

### Next Monday

- New feature launch
- Previous feature analytics review

## Video Production

### Demo Video Structure (2-3 minutes)

1. **Hook (0:00-0:15)** - Problem statement
2. **Solution (0:15-0:30)** - Feature introduction
3. **Demo (0:30-1:30)** - Live walkthrough
4. **Benefits (1:30-2:00)** - Value proposition
5. **CTA (2:00-2:15)** - Try it now
6. **Outro (2:15-2:30)** - Next week tease

### Production Quality

- 1080p resolution minimum
- Clear audio with background music
- On-screen text and highlights
- Axolop branding throughout
- Closed captions included

## Coordination Process

### Pre-Launch (Previous Week)

- **Monday** - Finalize marketing materials
- **Tuesday** - Prepare email sequences
- **Wednesday** - Schedule social posts

### Launch Day

- **8:45 AM** - Final systems check

- **9:15 AM** - Email blast sent
- **10:00 AM** - Social media posts live
- **11:00 AM** - Blog post published
- **2:00 PM** - Video demo released

### Post-Launch

- **Daily** - Monitor metrics and engagement
- **Tuesday** - Address user feedback
- **Wednesday** - Share user testimonials
- **Thursday** - Publish use case examples
- **Friday** - Weekly performance review

## Best Practices

### Do's

- Start with problem, then solution
- Use real customer testimonials
- Show, don't just tell
- Include clear CTAs in all content
- Respond to all comments and questions
- Track everything for optimization

### Don'ts

- Skip testing before launch
- Ignore negative feedback
- Overpromise on capabilities
- Forget to update documentation
- Neglect follow-up content
- Skip performance analysis
