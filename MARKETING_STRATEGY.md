# PART 8: VOICE & TONE GUIDELINES

## The Axolop Voice

### DO:
- Be funny without trying too hard
- Use internet-native language
- Call out industry BS
- Make fun of competitors (tastefully)
- Celebrate customer wins loudly
- Admit when we mess up
- Use "we" and "you" - never corporate speak

### DON'T:
- Be mean-spirited
- Punch down
- Use jargon nobody understands
- Be corporate or stiff
- Overpromise
- Ignore criticism
- Take ourselves too seriously

---

## Tone by Context

### Social Media (TOF)
**Energy Level:** 8/10
**Humor:** High
**Examples:**
- "Y'all really paying $500/month for a CRM that still can't send emails properly"
- "POV: explaining your tech stack to a new hire"

### Email Marketing
**Energy Level:** 7/10
**Humor:** Medium-High
**Examples:**
- "Your SaaS bill called. It's crying."
- "Quick question: how many passwords did you reset this week?"

### Product/Demo
**Energy Level:** 6/10
**Humor:** Medium
**Examples:**
- "Here's how to never lose client data again"
- "This feature will change how you work (for real though)"

### Support/Help
**Energy Level:** 5/10
**Humor:** Low (keep it helpful)
**Examples:**
- "Got you covered. Here's how to fix that..."
- "Weird bug! We're on it. Here's a workaround..."

### Crisis/Issues
**Energy Level:** 4/10
**Humor:** None
**Examples:**
- "We messed up. Here's what happened and what we're doing about it."
- "Downtime update: Back online. Full explanation below."

---

## Banned Phrases

Never use:
- "Synergy"
- "Circle back"
- "Low-hanging fruit"
- "At the end of the day"
- "Move the needle"
- "Revolutionary" (unless truly revolutionary)
- "Game-changer" (unless truly game-changing)
- "Best-in-class"
- "World-class"
- "Dear valued customer"

# PART 9: COMPETITOR POSITIONING

## How We Talk About Competitors

### HubSpot
**Our angle:** Overpriced, overcomplicated, overkill for agencies
**Line:** "HubSpot is great if you enjoy paying $1,800/month for features you'll never use"

### GoHighLevel
**Our angle:** Good concept, but still requires bolt-ons
**Line:** "GHL promised to replace everything. You're still using 5 other tools, aren't you?"

### Salesforce
**Our angle:** Enterprise dinosaur, not built for agencies
**Line:** "Salesforce is what happens when you need 10 consultants to use your CRM"

### ClickUp/Monday/Asana
**Our angle:** Great for project management, but now you need 5 more tools
**Line:** "Awesome! Now where's your CRM? And email? And forms? And..."

### Notion
**Our angle:** Beautiful, but not a CRM
**Line:** "We love Notion. It's just not a CRM, no matter how many templates you download"

---

## Comparison Framework

When comparing, always:
1. Acknowledge competitor strengths
2. Point out the gap we fill
3. Show the cost difference
4. Never lie or exaggerate
