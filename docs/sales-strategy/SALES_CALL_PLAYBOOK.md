## THE PERFECT SALES CALL STRUCTURE

**Total Time:** 45-60 minutes

| Phase | Time | Purpose |
|-------|------|---------|
| Rapport | 5 min | Build trust, human connection |
| Set Agenda | 2 min | Control the call, set expectations |
| Discovery | 20 min | Find pain, understand situation |
| Demo | 15 min | Show solution to THEIR problems |
| Objections | 10 min | Handle resistance |
| Close | 8 min | Get the commitment |

---

## PHASE 1: RAPPORT (5 Minutes)

### Opening Script

```
"Hey {{first_name}}! Thanks for jumping on. How's your [day/week] going so far?"

[Let them answer - mirror their energy]

"Awesome. Before we dive in, I have to ask - I saw on your LinkedIn that you're in {{city}}. How's the weather there?"

[Small talk - 2-3 minutes max]

"Perfect. Well, I'm excited to chat. I think there might be a good fit here, but we'll find out together."
```

### Rapport Tips

**DO:**
- Find common ground (city, industry, mutual connections)
- Match their energy level (upbeat = upbeat, calm = calm)
- Be genuine - people smell fake from a mile away
- Use their name 2-3 times in the first 5 minutes

**DON'T:**
- Rush past rapport (you'll pay for it in the close)
- Make it awkward (don't force connection)
- Talk about yourself too much
- Go longer than 5 minutes

---

## PHASE 2: SET AGENDA (2 Minutes)

### Agenda Script

```
"Alright {{first_name}}, here's how I like to run these calls - and stop me if this doesn't work for you:

First, I'm going to ask you a bunch of questions about your current situation. Not to be nosy, but to see if we're actually a fit.

Then, if it makes sense, I'll show you exactly how Axolop works and how it could help.

After that, we'll decide together if it makes sense to move forward.

Fair?"
```

**Why This Works:**
- You're in control (they know what to expect)
- You've planted the idea of "moving forward"
- You've set up that there might NOT be a fit (takes pressure off)
- They agreed to the process

## PHASE 3: DISCOVERY (20 Minutes)

### The SPIN Framework

**S** - Situation Questions (understand current state)
**P** - Problem Questions (find the pain)
**I** - Implication Questions (make the pain worse)
**N** - Need-Payoff Questions (make them want the solution)

---

### Situation Questions (5 min)

**Purpose:** Understand their current state

```
"Walk me through your current sales process. How do you track leads right now?"

"What CRM are you using? [Or: Are you using any software to manage this?]"

"How many leads are you handling per month, roughly?"

"How big is your sales team?"

"What's working well about your current setup?"
```

**Notes:**
- Don't spend too long here
- Get the facts, move to problems
- Listen for hints of pain

---

### Problem Questions (5 min)

**Purpose:** Find the pain points

```
"What's the most frustrating part about your current system?"

"Have you ever lost a deal because someone forgot to follow up?"

"How often does that happen?"

"What happens when a lead falls through the cracks?"

"If you could wave a magic wand and fix ONE thing about your sales process, what would it be?"
```

**Listen for:**
- Specific examples (stories)
- Emotional language (frustrated, annoying, hate)
- Frequency (always, every week, constantly)
- Cost (lost deals, lost money, lost time)

---

### Implication Questions (5 min)

**Purpose:** Make the pain worse (they need to FEEL it)

```
"You said you lose 2-3 deals per month to poor follow-up. What's your average deal size?"

[Wait for answer]

"So that's... $20-30k per month walking out the door? $240-360k per year?"

"How does that affect [their specific goal - growth, hiring, stress]?"

"What happens if you don't fix this in the next 6 months?"

"How does your team feel when they lose a deal they should have closed?"
```

**Key:** Let the silence sit. Let them FEEL the weight of the problem.

---

### Need-Payoff Questions (5 min)

**Purpose:** Make them want the solution (before you present it)

```
"If you never lost another deal to poor follow-up, what would that mean for your business?"

"If you could get 10 hours per week back from manual work, what would you do with that time?"

"What if you knew exactly which leads to call first, every single day?"

"If you could consolidate all your tools into one platform and save $1,000/month, where would that money go?"

"What does success look like for you in the next 90 days?"
```

**Key:** Make them paint the picture of success. Now they want it.

---

### Discovery Summary (1 min)

**Before moving to demo, summarize what you heard:**

```
"Okay {{first_name}}, let me make sure I got this right:

Right now you're using [current solution], which is costing you [pain point 1].

You're losing about [X deals/month] because [pain point 2].

That's about [$X/year] in lost revenue.

And your main goal is to [their stated goal].

Did I miss anything?"

[Let them confirm or add]

"Perfect. Let me show you exactly how Axolop solves this."
```

## PHASE 4: DEMO (15 Minutes)

### Demo Rules

**Rule #1:** Only show features that solve THEIR problems.
**Rule #2:** After each feature, ask "Does this solve [their problem]?"
**Rule #3:** Keep it short. 15 minutes max.
**Rule #4:** Let them talk. Ask questions throughout.

---

### Demo Structure

**Connect Each Feature to Their Pain:**

```
"You said you're losing deals because of poor follow-up. Here's how we solve that..."

[Show feature]

"See this? The AI scores every lead 1-100. You told me you have 200 leads and don't know who to call first. This tells you exactly who to call - RIGHT NOW."

[Show the score]

"Does this solve that problem for you?"

[Wait for answer - they should say yes]

"Great. Next problem you mentioned..."
```

---

### Feature-to-Problem Mapping

| Their Problem | Feature to Show | What to Say |
|---------------|-----------------|-------------|
| Losing deals to poor follow-up | AI reminders, lead scoring | "The AI flags deals before they go cold. You said you forgot to call Marcus. This would have reminded you." |
| Too many tools, too expensive | Dashboard, integrations | "This replaces your [list their tools]. One platform. One login. $99/month instead of $1,500." |
| Don't know who to call | AI lead scoring | "Every lead gets a score 1-100. Call the 90s first, nurture the 30s. Science, not guessing." |
| Emails take too long | AI email writer | "Click this button. Tell it what you need. Watch. [AI generates email] 5 seconds." |
| Team isn't on same page | Pipeline, activity tracking | "Everyone sees the same pipeline. Every call logged. No more 'did you call them?' conversations." |
| Data scattered everywhere | Contact 360 view | "Everything in one place. Emails, calls, notes, deals. Full history at a glance." |

---

### Demo Check-Ins

**After each major feature:**

```
"Does this make sense?"

"Can you see how this would help with [their specific problem]?"

"Any questions about this part?"

"On a scale of 1-10, how valuable is this for you?"
```

---

### Demo Close

Quick recap:
- AI lead scoring so you know who to call [solves their problem 1]
- Automatic follow-up reminders so you never forget [solves their problem 2]
- One platform replacing [their current tools] [solves their problem 3]

## PHASE 5: OBJECTION HANDLING (10 Minutes)

### The LAER Framework

**L** - Listen (let them finish completely)
**A** - Acknowledge (validate their concern)
**E** - Explore (understand the real objection)
**R** - Respond (address it with proof)

---

## CALL RECORDING & REVIEW

### What to Track

After every call, record:
- [ ] Did I build rapport? (Rating 1-10)
- [ ] Did I complete full discovery? (Y/N)
- [ ] Did I tie demo to their specific pains? (Y/N)
- [ ] What objections came up?
- [ ] How did I handle them?
- [ ] Did I close? (Y/N)
- [ ] If no, why not?
- [ ] What could I do better?

### Weekly Review

Listen to 2-3 calls per week. Focus on:
- Discovery quality (did I ask the right questions?)
- Objection handling (could I have done better?)
- Close attempts (did I actually ask for the sale?)
- Talk ratio (was I listening more than talking?)
